Project 03

Sound + Vision

National Science and Media Museum, 2025

Client
National Science and Media Museum
Funders
National Lottery Heritage Fund, Bradford 2025
Role
Marketing Manager (Co-Lead)
Year
2025

Sound and Vision is the culmination of a multi-million pound transformation of the National Science and Media Museum. Two new permanent galleries spanning two floors showcase over 500 world-class exhibits from the museum's collection of photography, film, television, animation, video games and sound technologies.

Working in a job share arrangement, I co-led the campaign with my partner, focusing on creative delivery and production. This meant turning the ambition of the galleries into campaign materials that could communicate the scale, diversity and local relevance of what we were launching.

I managed the campaign planning and content strategy, working with design agencies to develop assets that captured both the historical significance and contemporary energy of the collections. The galleries opened just days after installation was completed, so coordinating photo and video shoots required tight timelines and quick turnarounds.

I secured print and out-of-home advertising across Yorkshire, working directly with suppliers like Pop Art Media (poster towers in Leeds) and Metro newspaper to extend our local reach. I commissioned photography that ended up being picked up by BBC and regional media, helping to amplify the story beyond our owned channels.
