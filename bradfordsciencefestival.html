Project 01

Bradford Science Festival

National Science and Media Museum, 2025

Client
National Science and Media Museum
Role
Marketing Manager, Campaign Lead
Scope
Print, Digital OOH, Paid Social, PR
Year
2025

Bradford Science Festival 2025 brought nine days of free science programming across five venues throughout Bradford, running from 24th October to 1st November. The festival was strategically timed to align with Bradford's year as UK City of Culture.

The festival aimed to make science accessible to audiences who don't typically engage with STEM content, with particular focus on reaching communities in Bradford's BD3 and BD5 postal districts. Programming spanned four themes: Future Tech, Future Food, Future Climate, and Future Fashion, alongside standout experiences including Virtual Reality Antarctica, Living Dots interactive installations, and Born in Bradford data exhibitions.

As Marketing Manager, I led the integrated marketing campaign, developing and executing strategy across design, print, digital advertising, social media and content partnerships. I managed campaign budget allocation, coordinated with external agencies including AKA for media buying and How Do Yorkshire for content creation, and worked with Different PR on Born in Bradford press activity.

Campaign Deliverables

  • Print distribution to 513 venues across Yorkshire
  • Half-page advertisement in Primary Times
  • Digital OOH: 3 city centre screens, 2 roadside ads, 1 rail station screen
  • Paid social campaign with 7 distinct ad sets
  • Content partnership with How Do Yorkshire
  • 13 organic posts generating 39,055 views
  • Press coverage including BBC News Online
  • Isometric illustration system across all touchpoints

Results

  • 20% visitors with low science capital (vs usual 6%)
  • 38% minority ethnic attendees (vs usual 25%)
  • 61% Bradford postcodes (vs usual 29%)
  • 100% visitor enjoyment rating
  • 290,000+ paid social reach
  • 42 million total press reach
